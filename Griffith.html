IAS 38: Internally developed CRM capitalisation
AED 240K of annual freelancer development cost previously expensed has been capitalised as an internally generated intangible, amortised over a 3 year useful life. Recognition criteria under IAS 38.57 are met: technical feasibility, intention to complete, and reliable measurement of directly attributable cost. Effect is an uplift to reported EBITDA and a strengthened intangible asset base.
IAS 2: Net realisable value test
Inventory carried at the lower of cost and NRV. A writedown of AED 0.62M has been recognised, representing 2.2% of gross inventory, driven by slow moving SKUs in the Egypt depot approaching shelf life thresholds. Provision policy is 50% at 6 months to expiry and 100% at 3 months.
IFRS 15: Variable consideration on trade rebates
Promotional rebates and volume incentives are treated as variable consideration and netted against revenue using the expected value method. Accrual stands at AED 8.2M. The constraint test confirms no material reversal of cumulative revenue is probable.
IFRS 9: Expected credit loss on receivables
A simplified lifetime ECL model is applied to trade receivables using an aging based provision matrix. Provision of AED 2.1M covers 3.5% of gross AR. The over 90 day bucket at 6.2% is the primary driver and carries a 45% loss rate assumption.
